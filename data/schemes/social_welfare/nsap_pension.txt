Scheme Name: National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP)

What It Provides:
A social safety net providing pensions and financial assistance to elderly, widows, and disabled persons from BPL families. The programme includes multiple components:
- Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS): Rs 200/month for BPL persons aged 60-79, Rs 500/month for 80+.
- Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme (IGNWPS): Rs 300/month for BPL widows aged 40-79, Rs 500/month for 80+.
- Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (IGNDPS): Rs 300/month for BPL persons aged 18-79 with severe/multiple disabilities, Rs 500/month for 80+.
- National Family Benefit Scheme (NFBS): Lump sum of Rs 20,000 to BPL families on the death of the primary breadwinner.
States may add their own top-up amounts to the central government contribution.

Eligibility:
Old Age Pension: BPL persons aged 60 years and above.
Widow Pension: BPL widows aged 40 years and above.
Disability Pension: BPL persons aged 18-79 with 80% or more disability.
NFBS: BPL family where the primary breadwinner (aged 18-59) has died.

Required Documents:
1. Age proof (Aadhaar, birth certificate, school certificate, or medical officer's certificate)
2. BPL certificate or ration card or SECC data verification
3. Aadhaar card
4. Bank/post office account details
5. Widow certificate / death certificate of spouse (for widow pension)
6. Disability certificate from competent medical authority (for disability pension)
7. Death certificate of breadwinner (for NFBS)
8. Passport-size photograph

How to Apply:
1. Obtain the application form from your local Gram Panchayat, Block/Taluka office, or district social welfare office.
2. Fill the form and attach self-attested copies of required documents.
3. Submit the application at the Gram Panchayat or Urban Local Body office.
4. The application is verified by local authorities and forwarded to the district level.
5. Upon approval, the pension is credited monthly to the beneficiary's bank or post office account.

Where to Apply:
Gram Panchayat, Block Development Office, District Social Welfare Office, or through state-specific online portals where available. Central portal: nsap.nic.in.
