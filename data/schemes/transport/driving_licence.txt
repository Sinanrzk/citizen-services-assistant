Scheme Name: Driving Licence Application

What It Provides:
A Driving Licence (DL) is an official document issued by the Regional Transport Office (RTO) permitting a person to operate motor vehicles on public roads. The process includes obtaining a Learner's Licence (LL) first, followed by a permanent Driving Licence after a mandatory learning period of 30 days. Licences are issued for various vehicle categories including two-wheelers, four-wheelers (LMV), and commercial vehicles (HMV/Transport).

Eligibility:
Learner's Licence: Minimum age 16 years for two-wheelers without gear (below 50cc) and 18 years for motorcycles and cars. For commercial/transport vehicles: minimum 20 years with a valid LMV licence. The applicant must not be disqualified by any court and must be physically and mentally fit to drive.

Required Documents:
1. Proof of age (birth certificate, 10th mark sheet, passport, or Aadhaar card)
2. Proof of address (Aadhaar, utility bill, voter ID, passport)
3. Passport-size photographs
4. Medical certificate in Form 1-A (for transport licence)
5. Learner's Licence (for permanent DL application)

How to Apply:
1. Visit parivahan.gov.in/sarathi and fill the online application form (Form 2 for LL, Form 4 for DL).
2. Book a slot at your nearest RTO.
3. Pay the applicable fee online (Rs 200 for LL, Rs 200 for DL test, plus smart card fee).
4. Visit the RTO on the scheduled date with original documents.
5. Pass the computer-based test for Learner's Licence.
6. After 30 days, appear for the driving test at the RTO for permanent DL.
7. Upon passing, the Driving Licence is dispatched to your address.

Where to Apply:
Online at parivahan.gov.in/sarathi or visit your nearest Regional Transport Office (RTO).
