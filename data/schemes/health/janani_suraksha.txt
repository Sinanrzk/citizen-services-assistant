Scheme Name: Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)

What It Provides:
Cash assistance to pregnant women for institutional delivery (delivery at a hospital or health centre) to reduce maternal and neonatal mortality. Benefits include Rs 1,400 for rural mothers and Rs 1,000 for urban mothers in Low Performing States (LPS). In High Performing States (HPS), Rs 700 is given to rural BPL mothers. ASHA workers also receive incentives for facilitating institutional deliveries.

Eligibility:
All pregnant women delivering in government or accredited private health facilities are eligible in LPS states. In HPS states, benefits are limited to BPL and SC/ST women. The woman must be 19 years or older and the benefit covers up to two live births.

Required Documents:
1. JSY registration card (issued at the health facility or by ASHA worker)
2. Aadhaar card or government photo ID
3. BPL certificate or SC/ST certificate (for HPS states)
4. Bank account details for Direct Benefit Transfer
5. Delivery discharge slip from the health facility

How to Apply:
1. Register with your local ASHA worker or ANM during pregnancy.
2. Attend at least three ante-natal check-ups.
3. Deliver at a registered government or accredited private health facility.
4. Submit required documents post-delivery for cash transfer.
5. Cash benefit is transferred directly to your bank account.

Where to Apply:
Nearest government health centre, PHC, CHC, district hospital, or through your local ASHA worker.
