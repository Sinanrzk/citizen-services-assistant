Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) — Mudra Loan

What It Provides:
Collateral-free loans for micro and small enterprises under three categories: Shishu (loans up to Rs 50,000), Kishore (loans from Rs 50,001 to Rs 5 lakh), and Tarun (loans from Rs 5,00,001 to Rs 10 lakh). The scheme supports non-corporate, non-farm small and micro enterprises including manufacturing, trading, services, and allied activities. No processing fee is charged and repayment tenure is flexible (up to 5-7 years).

Eligibility:
Any Indian citizen with a business plan for a non-farm income-generating activity can apply. Eligible borrowers include small shopkeepers, fruit/vegetable vendors, artisans, small manufacturers, food service units, repair shops, machine operators, and other micro-enterprises. Both new and existing businesses can apply. No collateral or guarantor is required.

Required Documents:
1. Identity proof (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving Licence)
2. Address proof (Aadhaar, utility bill, or rental agreement)
3. Passport-size photographs
4. Business plan or project report (brief description of the activity)
5. Proof of business existence (registration, licence, or Udyam certificate if available)
6. Quotation for machinery/equipment (if applicable)
7. Bank account statements for the last 6 months (for existing businesses)

How to Apply:
1. Visit any scheduled commercial bank, regional rural bank, small finance bank, NBFC-MFI, or micro-finance institution.
2. Request the Mudra Loan application form.
3. Submit the form along with the required documents and business plan.
4. The bank evaluates the application and sanctions the loan.
5. Alternatively, apply online through the Udyamimitra portal (udyamimitra.in) or Mudra's website (mudra.org.in).

Where to Apply:
Any bank branch, NBFC-MFI, or online at udyamimitra.in.
