Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) — Skill Development

What It Provides:
Free skill development training and certification aligned with industry standards under the National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF). Trainees receive a government-recognised certificate upon successful assessment, along with placement assistance. Training costs, assessment fees, and certification charges are borne by the government. A monetary reward of Rs 8,000 on average is provided to successfully certified candidates under the Short-Term Training component.

Eligibility:
Indian nationals who are school/college dropouts or unemployed youth. Candidates must hold an Aadhaar card and a valid bank account. Age requirements vary by trade/sector but typically range from 15 to 45 years. No minimum educational qualification is required for most courses. Prior learning can also be recognised through the Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) component.

Required Documents:
1. Aadhaar card
2. Bank account details
3. Passport-size photographs
4. Educational certificates (if available)
5. Address proof

How to Apply:
1. Visit the PMKVY portal at pmkvyofficial.org and explore available training centres and courses.
2. Locate a PMKVY-affiliated training centre near you using the centre locator tool.
3. Visit the centre and enrol for your preferred skill course.
4. Complete the training duration (typically 150-300 hours).
5. Appear for the assessment conducted by a Sector Skill Council assessor.
6. Upon passing, receive the government certification and placement support.

Where to Apply:
PMKVY training centres, Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Kendras (PMKK), or online at pmkvyofficial.org.
