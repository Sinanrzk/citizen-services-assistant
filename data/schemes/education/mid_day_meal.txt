Scheme Name: Mid-Day Meal Scheme (PM POSHAN — Pradhan Mantri Poshan Shakti Nirman)

What It Provides:
Free cooked nutritious meals to children studying in Classes I to VIII in government and government-aided schools on every school working day. The meal provides a minimum of 450 calories and 12 grams of protein for primary classes, and 700 calories with 20 grams of protein for upper primary classes. The scheme also aims to improve school attendance, reduce dropout rates, and address malnutrition among children.

Eligibility:
All children enrolled in Classes I to VIII in government schools, government-aided schools, local body schools, Education Guarantee Scheme centres, and Madrasa/Maqtab schools supported under Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan are automatically eligible. No separate application is needed — benefits are provided universally to enrolled students.

Required Documents:
No documents required from parents or guardians. The school enrolment record serves as the basis for the meal provision.

How to Apply:
1. Enrol your child in a government or government-aided school.
2. The school automatically provides mid-day meals to all enrolled students.
3. Parents do not need to apply separately.
4. Meal quality and distribution are monitored by School Management Committees (SMC).
5. Complaints or feedback can be submitted to the school headmaster or District Education Officer.

Where to Apply:
No application needed. Meals are provided at the school level. For grievances, contact the school administration or the Mid-Day Meal authority in your district.
