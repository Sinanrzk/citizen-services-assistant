Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)

What It Provides:
Free LPG (cooking gas) connections to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households to replace unclean cooking fuels like firewood, coal, and dung. The scheme provides a financial support of Rs 1,600 per connection, which covers the security deposit for the cylinder, pressure regulator, and booklet. Under PMUY 2.0, the first LPG refill and a hotplate are also provided free of cost.

Eligibility:
Women belonging to BPL households as per the SECC-2011 data. The applicant must be a woman above 18 years of age. The household should not have an existing LPG connection. Priority is given to SC/ST families, beneficiaries of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY-G), Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY), forest dwellers, tea garden/ex-tea garden tribes, and inhabitants of islands and river islands.

Required Documents:
1. BPL certificate or name in SECC-2011 list
2. Aadhaar card of the applicant (adult woman)
3. Aadhaar card of all household members
4. Passport-size photograph
5. Bank account details (for subsidy transfer)
6. Ration card or address proof
7. Self-declaration (14-point declaration form)

How to Apply:
1. Visit the nearest LPG distributor (HP, Bharat Gas, or Indane).
2. Fill the Ujjwala application form (KYC form + 14-point declaration).
3. Submit the form with required documents.
4. The distributor verifies eligibility against the SECC database.
5. Upon approval, the LPG connection is installed at the applicant's residence.

Where to Apply:
Nearest LPG distributor, Common Service Centre, or contact the Ujjwala helpline at 1906. Details at pmuy.gov.in.
