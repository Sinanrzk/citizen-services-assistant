Scheme Name: Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

What It Provides:
A government-backed savings scheme exclusively for the girl child, offering one of the highest interest rates among small savings instruments (currently around 8.2% per annum, revised quarterly). The account matures after 21 years from the date of opening or upon marriage of the girl after age 18. Deposits qualify for tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, and the interest earned and maturity amount are fully tax-free (EEE status). Partial withdrawal of up to 50% is allowed after the girl turns 18 for higher education expenses.

Eligibility:
Parents or legal guardians of a girl child below 10 years of age can open the account. A maximum of two SSY accounts can be opened — one for each girl child (exception: second account before birth of twin girls, or in case of triplets). The girl must be an Indian citizen or resident.

Required Documents:
1. Birth certificate of the girl child
2. Identity proof of the parent/guardian (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID)
3. Address proof of the parent/guardian (Aadhaar, utility bill, or Passport)
4. Passport-size photographs of the girl child and parent/guardian
5. Medical certificate from the hospital (for newborns)

How to Apply:
1. Visit any post office or authorised commercial bank (SBI, PNB, BOB, ICICI, Axis, etc.).
2. Obtain and fill the Sukanya Samriddhi Account opening form.
3. Submit the form with the girl child's birth certificate and parent's KYC documents.
4. Make the initial deposit (minimum Rs 250, maximum Rs 1.5 lakh per financial year).
5. The passbook is issued immediately upon account opening.
6. Deposits must be made for a minimum of 15 years; the account matures after 21 years.

Where to Apply:
Any India Post office or authorised scheduled commercial bank branch. For details, visit the India Post website or your bank's branch.
