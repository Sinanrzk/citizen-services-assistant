Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

What It Provides:
A national financial inclusion initiative that enables every unbanked household to open a zero-balance savings bank account. Benefits include a free RuPay debit card, accidental insurance cover of Rs 2 lakh, life insurance cover of Rs 30,000 (for accounts opened before a specified period), and an overdraft facility of up to Rs 10,000 for eligible account holders. Mobile banking and direct benefit transfer services are also available.

Eligibility:
Any Indian citizen above the age of 10 years who does not have an existing bank account can open a PMJDY account. Minor accounts (10-18 years) can be operated by a natural guardian. Small accounts can be opened even without officially valid documents, subject to conditions.

Required Documents:
1. Aadhaar card (serves as both identity and address proof — simplifies KYC)
2. If Aadhaar is not available: any one officially valid document — Voter ID, Driving Licence, PAN card, Passport, NREGA Job Card
3. Passport-size photograph
4. For small accounts (without OVD): self-attested photograph and signature/thumb impression verified by bank official

How to Apply:
1. Visit your nearest bank branch (any nationalised or scheduled commercial bank, or regional rural bank).
2. Request the PMJDY account opening form.
3. Fill in the form with personal details and submit with Aadhaar or other KYC documents.
4. The account is opened immediately with zero balance.
5. A RuPay debit card is issued along with the passbook.

Where to Apply:
Any bank branch, Banking Correspondent (BC) agents, or selected Common Service Centres. Visit pmjdy.gov.in for details.
